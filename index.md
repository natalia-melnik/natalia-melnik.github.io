

I am a PhD candidate working at the [Psychophysics of Appearance Laboratory](http://appearancelab.org/), a research laboratory at the [Institute of Psychology](https://www.psy.unibe.ch/), [University of Bern](https://www.unibe.ch/) (CH). I am fascinated about “everything” in the periphery! My research is focused on understanding shape and object perception in the periphery by using psychophysics and appearance-based methods. 

I received a M.Sc. in Cognitive Science in 2015. During the studies I was involved in EEG research, working on the suppression of semantic interference during an auditory working memory task.
